Shapes & Text

A simple page of black and white geometry.

The circle has no corners. It rolls on forever without stopping.

The square is stable. Four equal sides, four right angles.

The triangle is the strongest shape. It does not bend under pressure.

The diamond is just a square that decided to stand on its corner.

The oval is a circle that got stretched. It keeps things interesting.

The ring is a circle with nothing in the middle. Absence as form.

Everything is made of shapes.

Look around. Rectangles in your windows, circles in your coffee cup, triangles holding up the roof.

On Simplicity

There is something satisfying about black and white. No decisions about color. No gradients. Just contrast. Light and dark. Present and absent. That is all you need to make something visible.

On Geometry

Geometry is the language of space. Every building, every screen, every page is built on invisible lines and angles. These shapes are not decoration. They are the structure underneath everything.

Just shapes and text. Nothing more.
